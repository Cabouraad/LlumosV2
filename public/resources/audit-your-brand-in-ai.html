Why AI Brand Audits Matter

Traditional SEO audits focus on Google rankings and website traffic. But today, potential customers are increasingly asking AI assistants for recommendations before they ever reach a search engine. When someone asks ChatGPT "What's the best project management tool for small teams?" or Perplexity "Which CRM has the best automation features?"â€”is your brand in the answer?

An AI brand audit reveals:

  • Current visibility: How often AI platforms mention your brand
  • Positioning accuracy: Whether AI describes your offering correctly
  • Competitive gaps: Where competitors appear but you don't
  • Recommendation frequency: How often you're the top suggestion

The 5-Step Audit Process

1Define 15-20 Queries Your Ideal Customers Ask

Start by mapping the questions your target audience asks when researching solutions. Include:

  • Comparison queries: "Best X vs Y for [use case]"
  • Category queries: "Top 10 [category] tools in 2026"
  • Problem-solution queries: "How to solve [specific problem]"
  • Recommendation queries: "What [product type] should I use for..."

2Test Each Query Across All Major AI Platforms

Run your queries through each major platform to capture variations:

  • ChatGPT (GPT-4): The largest user base, often trusted for recommendations
  • Claude: Known for nuanced, detailed responses
  • Perplexity: Provides citations, making it easier to trace sources
  • Google AI Overviews: Appears at the top of traditional search results

Pro tip: Test at different times of day and on different daysâ€”AI responses can vary.

3Document When You Appear and How You're Positioned

For each query, record:

  • Whether your brand was mentioned (yes/no)
  • Position in the response (1st, 2nd, 3rd, or in a list)
  • How your brand was described
  • Any inaccuracies or outdated information
  • Screenshot or copy of the full response

4Compare Your Visibility to Top 5 Competitors

Track competitor mentions with the same rigor. Build a comparison matrix showing:

  • Mention frequency across platforms
  • Average position when mentioned
  • Queries where competitors appear but you don't
  • Positioning differences (how they're described vs. you)

5Create an Action Plan to Address Visibility Gaps

Prioritize gaps based on business impact and create targeted content to improve visibility. Common actions include:

  • Creating authoritative content for queries where you're absent
  • Building citations on high-authority sources AI platforms trust
  • Correcting outdated information through public content updates
  • Improving structured data to help AI understand your offerings

Essential Prompts to Test

Here are category-specific prompt templates to start your audit:

For SaaS/Software Companies

  • "What's the best [category] software for [company size]?"
  • "Compare [your brand] vs [competitor] for [use case]"
  • "Which [category] tools have the best [feature]?"
  • "I need a [category] solution that integrates with [tool]. What do you recommend?"

For Service Businesses

  • "Best [service type] companies in [location]"
  • "How do I find a good [service provider type]?"
  • "What should I look for in a [service type] agency?"
  • "[Your brand] reviews and reputation"

For E-commerce/Products

  • "Best [product category] for [use case] in 2026"
  • "Is [your brand] worth buying?"
  • "[Your product] vs [competitor product] comparison"
  • "Where can I buy [product type] with [specific feature]?"

Competitor Analysis Framework

Use this framework to systematically compare your AI visibility against competitors:

  1. Identify your top 5 competitors (both direct and indirect)
  2. Run identical queries for each competitor you track
  3. Score each mention: 3 points for #1 position, 2 points for #2-3, 1 point for any mention
  4. Calculate share of voice (your points / total points Ã— 100)
  5. Identify gaps where competitors dominate but you're absent

Creating Your Action Plan

Based on your audit findings, prioritize actions by impact:

High Priority (Address First)

  • Queries with high search volume where you're completely absent
  • Inaccurate brand descriptions that could harm reputation
  • Comparison queries where competitors are favored

Medium Priority

  • Queries where you're mentioned but not prominently positioned
  • Missing features or product information in AI responses
  • Outdated pricing or offering details

Ongoing Optimization

  • Regular weekly or bi-weekly re-audits to track progress
  • New query discovery as customer language evolves
  • Citation building on authoritative sources
