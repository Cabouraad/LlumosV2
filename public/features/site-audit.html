What is Site Audit?

Site Audit is a comprehensive analysis tool that evaluates your website across the technical and content factors that influence AI search visibility. Unlike traditional SEO audits that focus solely on Google's ranking factors, our Site Audit examines the signals that large language models use when deciding which brands to recommend.

The audit crawls your website, analyzes each page, and generates a detailed report with weighted scores across six critical modules—each designed to surface the optimizations that matter most for generative engine optimization (GEO).

The Six Audit Modules

Crawl Health (20%)

Evaluates whether AI crawlers can effectively access and index your content. Includes robots.txt configuration, sitemap presence, and crawl error detection.

Performance (15%)

Measures page speed, mobile responsiveness, and Core Web Vitals—factors that influence both user experience and AI model confidence in your content.

On-Page SEO (15%)

Analyzes title tags, meta descriptions, heading structure, and content organization that help AI models understand your page topics.

Entity Optimization (20%)

Checks for structured data, schema markup, and brand entity signals that LLMs use to associate your website with specific topics and queries.

AI Readiness (20%)

Evaluates AI-specific factors including content freshness, llms.txt presence, clear content structure, and authoritative source signals.

Off-Site Signals (10%)

Assesses external factors like citation presence, backlink quality signals, and brand mentions that contribute to AI model trust.

Why Site Audit Matters for AI Visibility

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking in Google's ten blue links. But AI assistants work differently—they synthesize information from multiple sources to provide a single, authoritative answer. If your website isn't optimized for how AI models process content, you're invisible to a growing segment of searchers.

  • AI models prioritize fresh content. Our Content Freshness scoring identifies pages with outdated metadata, helping you maintain the recency signals that LLMs favor.
  • Structured data influences AI recommendations. Proper schema markup helps AI models understand your expertise, authoritativeness, and the specific topics you cover.
  • Technical barriers block AI crawlers. Many sites unknowingly block AI model crawlers or have configurations that prevent proper content indexing.
  • Entity signals build brand association. Clear brand entity markup strengthens the connection between your company and the topics you want to be known for.
  • Content structure affects comprehension. Well-organized content with clear headings and logical flow is more likely to be accurately understood and cited by AI.
